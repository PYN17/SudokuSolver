Sudoku Solver

Sudoku is a logic-based number placement puzzle that gained global popularity in the early 2000s, though its roots trace back much further. The modern version was popularized by the publisher Nikoli in 1984, with the name "Sudoku" meaning "single number" in Japanese. The puzzle consists of a 9×9 grid divided into nine 3×3 subgrids, where the objective is to fill each cell with digits 1 through 9 so that each row, column, and subgrid contains all the numbers without repetition. Despite its simple rules, Sudoku challenges players with increasingly complex logic and deduction, making it a timeless exercise in pattern recognition, reasoning, and constraint satisfaction.
